# Millings Conformance Standard™ (MCS v1.0)

**State Formation Governance & Decision Integrity Standard**  
**Author:** Frederick Redditt

---

## 1. Scope

This standard defines requirements for governing decision systems at the level of state formation.

---

## 2. Normative Principle

All downstream correctness is conditional on upstream state validity.

---

## 3. Definitions

**State Formation**  
Construction of internal system reality.

**State Admissibility**  
Conditions under which state is valid.

**Action Admissibility**  
Conditions under which action is allowed.

**Governance Drift**  
Misalignment between authority and decision production.

---

## 4. Requirements

### 4.1 State Formation Control  
Systems SHALL define state formation mechanisms.

### 4.2 Dual Admissibility  
Systems SHALL distinguish state vs action admissibility.

### 4.3 State Lineage  
Systems SHALL maintain state lineage traceability.

### 4.4 Re-evaluation Detection  
Systems SHALL detect re-evaluation traps.

### 4.5 Accountability Origin  
Systems SHALL align accountability with decision origin.

---

## 5. Failure Classes

- Mis-formed State  
- Admissible Falsehood  
- Proof Persistence  
- Governance Drift  
- Representational Bound Failure  

---

## 6. Conformance Levels

**Level 1:** Execution Governance  
**Level 2:** Admissibility Governance  
**Level 3:** State Formation Governance (MCS Compliant)

---

## 8. Final Doctrine

> Systems do not fail when they act.  
> They fail when mis-formed reality becomes admissible and persistent.
